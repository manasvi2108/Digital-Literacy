'Stay Safe Online' Checklist

As a Student Digital Ambassador, I have come up with this list of 8 tips for college students in India to stay safe online:

The Golden UPI Rule Never enter your UPI PIN for receiving money. You enter your PIN for sending or making a transaction, not for receiving a refund or winning a contest.

Verify Merchant's Identity

Before clicking "Pay" on apps like GPay or PhonePe, verify the actual name of the receiver as displayed on the app, not as displayed in the chat or SMS.

Two-Factor Authentication

Make sure your Gmail, GitHub, and other social media accounts are using two-factor authentication. Authenticator apps are more secure than SMS-based OTPs for authentication.

Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Transacting

Never use your bank account or transact using public Wi-Fi at cafes, airports, or hostels, even if it is for free.

Check App Permissions

Check your phone's app settings and revoke "Camera," "Microphone," or "Gallery" access for apps that do not require them.

SIM Card Security

Set a PIN for your SIM card. This way, even if your phone is stolen, the thief will not be able to use it for generating One Time Passwords for your bank

Identify Phishing Links:

Always check the URL before entering credentials. Look for https:// and ensure the domain name is spelled correctly (e.g., vith.ac.in vs v1th.ac.in).

Regular Software Updates:

Keep your mobile OS and banking apps updated to the latest version to protect against newly discovered security vulnerabilities.

How to Report Cybercrime in India:

If you encounter any online fraud or harassment, immediately use the official government channels:

National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal

National Helpline Number: 1930 (Available 24/7)
